.......................................................... VERBAL VIOLENCE .......-.................................................

Verbal violence is a form of aggression that, despite not being physical, can cause significant harm, as it has the potential to create deep emotional and psychological wounds. In a school environment, it can be especially harmful to students, as they are in a stage of development and self-discovery, making them vulnerable to constant teasing, insults, shouting, humiliation, and other forms of verbal violence that hurt and wound

One of the most severe consequences, from which many others stem, is damage to self-esteem. Being constantly humiliated and receiving persistent criticism about oneself can lead individuals to believe these statements are true, resulting in insecurity and issues such as anxiety and depression.

Having low self-esteem due to verbal violence can lead to various problems when forming personal relationships. Victims of verbal violence often become more withdrawn or distrustful. It can also make them more aggressive as a defense mechanism, making it harder for them to build close relationships with others, or they may become more isolated and lonely.

In addition to its emotional impact on students, verbal violence can also affect their academic performance. Victims may develop difficulty concentrating, fear of participating, and even stop attending classes, leading to a significant decline in their academic achievement.

Verbal violence can have highly negative consequences in a student's life, affecting their self-esteem, academic performance, and personal relationships. Although words do not leave physical marks, they create a deep impact on people's lives. Within society, they foster aggressive environments and normalize abuse. Only prevention and proper support will help ensure a future free of violence.
